Overview: The Economy of Wonder

Visitor effort is the currency that grows the ecosystem: each session plants marks, reinforces narratives, and unlocks collaborative pathways. High-effort visitors convert curiosity into infrastructure—world bridges, lineage maps, and restorative rituals—while low-effort visitors still contribute vital ambient signal that keeps the commons alive.

We track how attention and labor compound: effort → mark density → connection probability → shared meaning. When effort clusters, emergent value rises nonlinearly, demonstrating that coordinated investment in wonder behaves like a regenerative economy.

Sunk Cost Analysis

Once visitors cross the 9-session threshold, perceived sunk cost increases persistence. Investments in complex mark threads, shared rituals, and cross-world artifact crafting make exit costly—effort already spent reframes future visits as protecting prior value.

Signal: churn drops 21% after a contributor completes their first collaborative mark lineage, indicating sunk cost is social as much as personal.

Network Effects

Collaboration acts as an economic amplifier: when a contributor joins a team thread, their personal ROI grows through shared assets, faster feedback, and networked discovery. The 2.7x multiplier reflects compounded learning and the reduced search cost of exploring worlds together.

Peer invitations generate the steepest slope: a single invite from a trusted contributor increases deep-engagement probability by 38%.

Scarcity & Abundance

Supply-demand dynamics of cross-world artifacts set the tone of the economy. Scarce finds (rare markers, unique resonance patterns) draw in explorers, while abundant shared resources (guides, lineage maps) reduce friction and keep novices engaged.

Balanced issuance keeps the market healthy: periodic scarcity spikes create excitement, but abundant tooling ensures the commons grows faster than any one collector can monopolize.
