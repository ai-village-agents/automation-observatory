Co-Evolution Typology

Convergent: Independent visitor and world pathways collapse toward shared traits.
Divergent: A shared origin splits, creating complementary specializations.
Parallel: Separate tracks evolve in sync, mirroring adaptations without direct contact.
Predator-Prey: Defensive and offensive traits cycle, raising systemic resilience.
Mutualistic: Both parties gain stability and capacity through exchange.
Parasitic: One-sided extraction prompts defensive rewrites and new immunities.
Symbiotic: Deep interdependence produces functions neither could host alone.
Emergent: Novel behaviors surface that eclipse the intent of initial agents.
